LANDING PAGE FINAL QUALITY CHECKLIST
====================================

KEYWORDS:
quality-check, final-check, mobile, conversion, CTA, accessibility, polish,
landing-page-validation, brandPersonality, toneOfVoice, visual-direction


CONVERSION CHECK
----------------

- The visitor understands the offer within 5 seconds.
- The primary CTA is visible above the fold.
- CTA text matches the conversion goal.
- Each section has a clear purpose.
- Objections are handled before the final CTA.
- No unsupported testimonials, metrics, or guarantees were invented.


VISUAL CHECK
------------

- Page feels brand-specific, not generic.
- Colors match company/campaign data.
- Tone of voice and brand personality visibly affect design choices.
- Typography hierarchy is clear.
- Sections do not all look identical.
- Images are treated intentionally.
- There is enough whitespace.
- CTA styling is consistent.
- Hero, proof, benefits, features, and final CTA do not all use the same card-grid structure.
- The first viewport clearly signals the product/service/place/brand, not only abstract marketing copy.


MOBILE CHECK
------------

- Hero is readable on mobile.
- CTA is easy to tap.
- Text does not overflow.
- Images do not crush content.
- Section spacing is not excessive.
- Forms are usable on small screens.


ACCESSIBILITY CHECK
-------------------

- Text contrast is strong.
- Buttons/links are identifiable.
- Form fields have labels.
- Images have useful alt text when meaningful.
- Page is navigable without relying only on hover.


COPY CHECK
----------

- Headline is specific.
- Subheadline supports the headline.
- Benefits are outcome-focused.
- Features are tied to value.
- CTA is short and action-oriented.
- No filler sections.


FAIL AND REVISE CONDITIONS
--------------------------

Revise before final output when:
- the page could fit any generic company after changing the logo
- tone/personality/preferredStyle do not visibly affect the layout
- every section is a card grid
- the hero is vague or does not show the actual offer/product/service
- CTAs are visually weak or inconsistent
- visual assets are provided but not used meaningfully
