CHILIFORGE — FORM DECISIONS GUIDE
Last updated: 2026-05-28

This guide helps users understand each form field in ChiliForge and make good decisions
when filling out the Ad Creative or Landing Page generator forms.

=== CAMPAIGN STRATEGY ===

CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE
What do you want to achieve with this campaign?
- Awareness: Introduce your brand/product to new audiences. Focus on bold visuals and memorable brand elements. No hard sell. Best for: new product launches, brand building, entering a new market.
- Consideration: Get people interested enough to learn more. Drive clicks, video views, sign-ups. Best for: lead generation, webinar registrations, free trial offers, content downloads.
- Conversion: Drive a specific action (purchase, subscription, download). Use strong CTAs, urgency, and social proof. Best for: e-commerce sales, subscription sign-ups, limited-time offers.
- Retention: Re-engage existing customers. Use personalized messaging and loyalty rewards. Best for: win-back campaigns, upsell, renewal reminders, loyalty programs.

FUNNEL STAGE (ToFU / MoFU / BoFU)
The funnel stage describes where your audience is in the buying journey:
- Top of Funnel (ToFU / Awareness): Audience doesn't know you yet. Use broad appeal, problem-aware messaging, and emotional hooks. Avoid jargon or deep product details. Goal: generate interest.
- Middle of Funnel (MoFU / Consideration): Audience knows the problem and is evaluating solutions. Highlight differentiators, benefits, social proof, and comparisons. Offer demos, free trials, or gated content. Goal: build trust.
- Bottom of Funnel (BoFU / Conversion): Audience is ready to decide. Use strong CTAs, urgency, guarantees, and risk-reversal. Remove friction and overcome final objections. Goal: close the sale.

Tip: Matching objective to funnel stage matters. Awareness + ToFU = brand campaigns. Conversion + BoFU = direct response.

=== AUDIENCE & OFFER ===

VALUE PROPOSITION
The single most compelling reason your product exists. Answer: "Why should this specific person choose this product right now?"
Good format: "[Product] helps [audience] [achieve desired outcome] without [main obstacle]."
Example: "ChiliForge helps marketers create professional ad creatives in minutes without any design skills."
Tip: Focus on the transformation/outcome, not just the feature. "Lose 10kg in 30 days" beats "our diet program."

PAIN POINTS
Frustrations, problems, or fears your audience has RIGHT NOW that your product solves.
Be specific and emotional — not "doesn't have time" but "spends 3 hours manually editing banners only to get mediocre results."
Use the audience's own language, not marketing speak.
Examples: "Can't afford a designer," "Previous ads wasted budget with no conversions," "Overwhelmed by too many tools."

DESIRES / ASPIRATIONS
What your audience WANTS to achieve, be, have, or feel after using your product.
Not the feature, but the transformation: not "faster exports" but "launching campaigns before competitors."
Examples: "A business that runs without me," "More time for family," "Recognition as an expert in my niche."

PRODUCT / SERVICE NAME
Be specific. Use the exact name users will recognize.
If you're promoting a specific offer within a product, use the offer name. E.g., "Premium Plan — Annual" vs just "ChiliForge."

PRICING
Be specific. "$297/mo" is better than "affordable."
If you have multiple tiers, use the most relevant one for this campaign's audience.
Leave blank if you're not featuring price in the creative — e.g., awareness campaigns.

OFFER / PROMOTION
The main hook: what's the deal? "50% off this weekend only," "Free bonus course included," "Early-bird pricing."
This is what makes people act NOW vs later.

GUARANTEE
Reduces perceived risk for the buyer. Examples: "30-day money-back guarantee," "Cancel anytime," "See results in 7 days or full refund."
A strong guarantee can significantly increase conversion rates. Always use it if you have one.

SCARCITY
Creates urgency by limiting availability. Must be REAL — fake scarcity destroys trust and brand credibility.
Examples: "Only 12 spots left in the cohort," "Offer ends Sunday at midnight," "First 50 customers get 40% off."
If you don't have real scarcity, leave it blank and rely on the offer strength instead.

=== TONE & URGENCY ===

TONE OF VOICE
Choose the tone that matches your brand identity AND resonates with your specific audience:
- Conversational: friendly, natural, like a recommendation from a friend. Best for: lifestyle brands, consumer products, personal brands.
- Casual: relaxed, laid-back, uses informal language. Best for: youth-oriented brands, entertainment, wellness, apps.
- Inspirational: motivating, aspirational, focuses on transformation. Best for: fitness, coaching, personal development, causes.
- Authoritative: expert, data-driven, confident with facts. Best for: B2B, SaaS, finance, health, education.
- Formal: professional, polished, institutional language. Best for: enterprise, legal, financial services, medical.
- Urgent: time-sensitive, action-driven, FOMO-based. Use carefully — effective for limited promotions but fatigues audiences if overused.
- Empathetic: understanding, caring, acknowledges feelings first. Best for: mental health, social causes, healthcare, parenting.

URGENCY LEVEL
How much time pressure to apply in the creative:
- None: Evergreen creative, no time pressure. Good for brand awareness or always-on acquisition campaigns.
- Low: Subtle nudge ("while supplies last," "limited availability"). Good for conversion ads not tied to a specific promotion.
- Medium: Clear but not aggressive ("Limited time offer," "This week only," "Don't miss out"). Most common setting for conversion campaigns.
- High: Strong FOMO ("24 hours left," "Last chance," "Only 3 spots remaining"). Use for flash sales, countdowns, and hard deadline-driven offers.

=== AD FORMATS ===

Which format should I choose?
- Story / Reels (1080x1920, vertical 9:16): Best for mobile-first platforms: Instagram Stories, Facebook Stories, TikTok, Reels. Very high engagement. Focus on bold visuals and minimal text — you have 2-3 seconds. Works best with motion-like design, strong colors, and a clear CTA at the bottom.
- Square Post (1080x1080, 1:1): Versatile, works across feed placements on Instagram and Facebook. Safe default for social media campaigns.
- Landscape (1200x628, 1.91:1): Standard for Facebook/Instagram feed, LinkedIn feed, and Google Display. News-feed style, more room for copy and context.
- Reels/TikTok (1080x1920): Same dimensions as Story but optimized for TikTok and Instagram Reels feed context.
- Pinterest Pin (1000x1500, 2:3): Tall format optimized for Pinterest. Great for products with strong visual appeal: fashion, food, home decor, DIY.
- Twitter/X Post (1200x675, 16:9): Standard Twitter/X ad format. Works like a landscape card.
- LinkedIn Post (1200x627, ~1.91:1): LinkedIn's standard image ad. Professional context — use authoritative or formal tone.
- YouTube Thumbnail (1280x720, 16:9): Clickable thumbnail for YouTube. Should feature a face showing emotion, clear product, or compelling text hook. High contrast, easy to read at small size.
- Leaderboard (728x90): Top or bottom of desktop web pages. Very limited space — use brand name + one punchy message.
- Banner (468x60): Classic web display format. Minimal real estate — brand + call to action only.
- Medium Rectangle (300x250): The most common display format globally. High Google Display Network inventory. Works for most campaign types.
- Large Rectangle (336x280): Slightly larger rectangle. Good CTR on content pages.
- Half Page (300x600): Premium display placement. Large format allows for storytelling. Good for brand campaigns and detailed offers.
- Wide Skyscraper (160x600): Tall, narrow format for desktop sidebars. Long-form vertical storytelling.
- Mobile Banner (320x50): Very small mobile format. Simple brand + CTA only.

=== A/B TESTING ===

When to enable A/B testing:
- Use when you want to test different messaging angles for the same audience in the same campaign
- Most valuable for campaigns with significant budget where small conversion improvements matter
- Good when you're unsure between two positioning approaches (emotional vs rational, pain vs desire)
- Less necessary for quick exploratory generations or early-stage campaigns without traffic data

How many variants to choose:
- 2 variants: Compare 2 headline approaches head-to-head (e.g., pain-focused vs desire-focused, question vs statement)
- 3 variants: Add a third angle to widen the test (e.g., benefit-focused, pain-focused, and social-proof-focused)

What gets varied in ChiliForge:
A/B testing varies the headline and CTA text per variant while keeping the same visual layout. This isolates copy as the variable so you can clearly identify which message performs best.

=== BRAND COLORS ===

Primary Color: Your main brand color — typically used for main backgrounds, headline text, and CTA buttons. Should be immediately recognizable as "your brand."
Secondary Color: Supporting brand color — used for accents, dividers, secondary text, and complementary design elements.
Accent Color: High-attention color — used specifically for CTAs, price badges, offers, and elements you want people to notice first. Often a contrasting color.
Text Color: Body copy and label text color. Usually dark (#1F2937 or similar) on light backgrounds, light (#FFFFFF or similar) on dark backgrounds.
Background Color: The base canvas color of the ad creative. Determines the overall feel: dark backgrounds feel premium/bold, light backgrounds feel clean/professional.

Tip: Contrast is critical. Ensure your text color has sufficient contrast against your background (minimum 4.5:1 ratio for readability). Avoid combining colors that are too similar in brightness.

=== IMAGES ===

Logo: Upload your brand logo (PNG with transparent background strongly recommended). It will appear on all generated creatives automatically. Keep it clean — complex logos can get lost at banner sizes.
Product Image: The hero visual of your product or service. Higher resolution = better quality generation. Clean backgrounds or natural context shots both work well. The AI analyzes the image and incorporates it into the ad layout composition.

Tip: For image-mode ads, provide a product image that clearly shows what you're selling. Lifestyle images (showing the product in use) tend to perform better than plain product shots for social media.

=== CAMPAIGN NAMING ===
Name your campaign so you can find it weeks or months later when reviewing results.
Recommended format: "[Brand] — [Offer or Theme] — [Month Year]"
Examples:
- "ACME Fitness — Black Friday 50% Off — Nov 2026"
- "LegalPro — Lead Gen Q1 — Jan 2026"
- "StyleBox — Spring Collection — Mar 2026"

=== AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION ===
A brief profile of who will see this ad. Include:
- Demographics: age range, gender if relevant, location if specific
- Interests and behaviors: what they consume, follow, engage with
- Psychographics: mindset, values, aspirations
Example: "Digital entrepreneurs 25-40, interested in marketing automation, follow business/growth creators, want to scale without hiring a team."

=== AGE RANGE ===
The primary age range of your target audience for this specific campaign.
Be realistic — broader ranges (25-45) are common for general products; narrower ranges (18-24) for youth-specific offers.
This helps the AI calibrate language complexity, cultural references, and visual style appropriately.

=== GENDER ===
- All: No gender-specific targeting or messaging. Good default for most products.
- Female: Creative will be calibrated with messaging and visual cues that resonate more with female audiences.
- Male: Creative will use messaging and visual styles that tend to resonate more with male audiences.
Choose based on your actual customer data, not assumptions. Most products perform better with "All."
