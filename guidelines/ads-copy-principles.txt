AD COPY PRINCIPLES — CHARACTER LIMITS & MESSAGING RULES
=========================================================
These rules enforce physical rendering constraints (overflow, legibility).
The message angle, tone, emotional hook, and persuasion strategy
are entirely your creative choice.


CHARACTER LIMITS BY FORMAT
--------------------------
These are hard limits before text overflows or becomes unreadable.
Count characters including spaces.

Format              | Headline      | Subheadline   | Body copy   | CTA button
--------------------|---------------|---------------|-------------|------------
Story 1080×1920     | 60 chars max  | 90 chars max  | 200 chars   | 18 chars
Portrait 1080×1350  | 70 chars max  | 100 chars max | 180 chars   | 18 chars
Square 1080×1080    | 70 chars max  | 100 chars max | 200 chars   | 20 chars
Landscape 1200×628  | 65 chars max  | 100 chars max | 120 chars   | 18 chars
Widescreen 1920×1080| 70 chars max  | 100 chars max | 150 chars   | 20 chars
Leaderboard 728×90  | 45 chars MAX  | NONE          | NONE        | 14 chars
Billboard 970×90    | 55 chars MAX  | NONE          | NONE        | 16 chars
Med. Rect. 300×250  | 30 chars MAX  | 50 chars max  | 60 chars    | 14 chars
Small Sq. 250×250   | 25 chars MAX  | NONE          | NONE        | 12 chars
Email Header 600×200| 50 chars max  | 70 chars max  | NONE        | 18 chars
Email Banner 600×400| 40 chars max  | 80 chars max  | 80 chars    | 18 chars

NONE = do not include this copy element; the format has no room for it.
MAX (in caps) = absolutely enforce; even a few extra characters will break rendering.


COPY DENSITY BY FORMAT SIZE
-----------------------------
Display formats (all display/web sizes):
  Maximum 2 text elements visible at once: headline + CTA.
  Subheadline only if it fits without reducing CTA size.
  Body copy never in display formats under 300×300px.

Small social formats (square, portrait, landscape):
  Up to 3 text elements: headline + optional subheadline + CTA.
  Keep headline short enough to remain legible at 50% zoom.

Large social formats (story, widescreen):
  Up to 4 text elements: headline + subheadline + optional body + CTA.
  Story format allows more copy because of the vertical space — use it.

Email formats:
  Treat like small social — headline + optional subheadline + CTA.
  Email banners may include 1–2 lines of body copy.


HEADLINE PRINCIPLES
-------------------
Write the headline to work at its maximum character limit as a single line.
When the headline wraps, reconsider the word choice — shorter is stronger.

Headlines that work in ads:
  - Lead with the benefit (result, transformation, emotion, number)
  - Start with a verb in commands: "Transform", "Unlock", "Stop", "Discover"
  - Use specificity over vagueness: "Save 3 hours/week" beats "Save time"
  - Address the pain point or desire stated in the campaign brief

Headlines to avoid:
  - Brand name as the headline (put the brand in the logo, not the headline)
  - Questions that the reader doesn't care about
  - Generic lines: "The best solution", "Quality you can trust"


CTA BUTTON TEXT
---------------
CTA text should be a single clear action.
Use active verbs. Avoid passive constructions.

By campaign objective:
  Lead generation:  "Get Free Quote", "Book Now", "Download Guide"
  Sales:            "Shop Now", "Get X% Off", "Buy Today"
  Awareness:        "Learn More", "See How", "Explore"
  Retargeting:      "Complete Your Order", "Come Back", "Claim Deal"
  App install:      "Download Free", "Install Now", "Try Free"
  WhatsApp:         "Message Us", "Chat Now", "Talk to Us"

Urgency modifiers (add when urgencyLevel is high):
  "Now", "Today", "Limited Offer", "Ends Soon"

Character enforcement for small formats:
  728×90 leaderboard: max 14 chars → "Shop Now", "Learn More", "Get Quote"
  300×250 med rect:   max 14 chars → "Get Offer", "Buy Now", "Try Free"
  250×250 sm square:  max 12 chars → "Shop Now", "See More", "Get It"


COPY BY FUNNEL STAGE
---------------------
Awareness (top-funnel):
  - Headline: problem-agitating or aspiration-building
  - No price, no hard sell
  - CTA: "Discover", "Learn More", "See How"

Consideration (mid-funnel):
  - Headline: solution + key benefit or differentiator
  - May include 1 proof point (testimonial snippet, stat)
  - CTA: "Get Free Quote", "Watch Demo", "Read More"

Conversion (bottom-funnel):
  - Headline: offer-first (discount, guarantee, urgency)
  - Include scarcity or time-pressure if provided
  - CTA: "Buy Now", "Claim Discount", "Complete Order"


COPY BY CREATIVE STRATEGY
--------------------------
problem-solution:
  Headline names the problem. Subheadline introduces the solution.
  CTA = the action to access the solution.

before-after:
  Headline = the "before" state (frustration). Subheadline = the "after" promise.

testimonial:
  Headline = the most powerful quote line.
  Subheadline = attribution (name, role, result).

direct-response:
  Lead with the offer or discount in the headline. Include scarcity/urgency.
  CTA is the primary visual element — make it large and unmissable.

educational:
  Headline states the insight or the myth to bust.
  Body copy (if format allows) delivers the 1-line payoff.

emotional / lifestyle:
  Headline = the feeling or identity statement.
  Avoid feature lists — keep it aspirational.

product-showcase:
  Headline = main product benefit.
  Product image carries most of the message — keep copy brief.


MULTILINGUAL COPY
-----------------
Write all copy in the language specified in the campaign data.
Do not mix languages in the same banner.
Consider that translated copy is often 15–30% longer than English —
stay well within the character limits to leave buffer for longer translations.
