AD VISUAL DESIGN SYSTEM
=======================

KEYWORDS:
preferredStyle, colors, typography, hierarchy, logo-strategy, image-treatment,
cta-style, premium, modern, minimal, bold, playful, corporate, luxury, tech,
toneOfVoice, brandPersonality, brandKeywords, art-direction


CORE QUALITY BAR
----------------

The banner must look like a finished paid ad, not a rough layout or miniature
landing page. Use a clear focal point, decisive spacing, high contrast, and a
CTA that feels intentionally designed.

The company tone and brand personality must change the visual direction, not
only the wording. Translate personality into typography, spacing, image crop,
CTA shape, amount of contrast, decorative energy, and composition.


COLOR USE
---------

Campaign/company colors are mandatory when provided.

Primary color:
- large brand expression
- background block, overlay, major shape, or dominant accent

Secondary color:
- supporting surfaces, dividers, panels, depth shapes

Accent color:
- CTA and conversion highlights
- do not scatter accent everywhere

Text color:
- use only when contrast is strong
- otherwise choose white or near-black for readability


VISUAL HIERARCHY
----------------

Every banner needs one dominant focal point.

Hierarchy order:
1. focal visual or headline
2. main headline
3. offer/proof/subheadline
4. CTA
5. logo/brand mark

CTA is important but should not visually fight with the headline or product.


IMAGE TREATMENT
---------------

Provided images should be treated like ad assets:
- crop intentionally
- use masks, panels, shadows, or overlays
- create depth with foreground/background layering
- avoid dropping images flat in the center without design treatment

First classify the image role:
- logo
- product packshot
- lifestyle/person
- UI screenshot
- texture/background
- abstract brand image

Then choose a treatment that fits the role. Product and hero images should use
one deliberate art-direction technique: floating cutout, masked frame, angled
card, side crop, corner anchor, split-panel hero, or centered showcase with
depth. Background images need a readability system such as gradient overlay,
solid text panel, vignette, blur panel, or split layout.

If the image is low quality, make it smaller, mask it, or use it as a textured
background with overlay. Do not stretch or pixelate it.

TONE AND PERSONALITY TRANSLATION
--------------------------------

toneOfVoice=confident
- assertive headline scale
- strong CTA contrast
- decisive geometric structure
- avoid timid small type

toneOfVoice=friendly
- approachable spacing
- warmer shapes
- softer CTA corners
- clear benefit-led copy

toneOfVoice=premium
- restrained copy density
- elegant negative space
- refined typography contrast
- image crop should feel editorial

toneOfVoice=urgent
- sharper CTA treatment
- visible offer/urgency badge
- high contrast and direct hierarchy
- avoid luxury-level understatement

brandPersonality=modern
- crisp panels, confident whitespace, clean crop

brandPersonality=playful
- asymmetry, expressive badges, rounded or unexpected shapes

brandPersonality=trustworthy
- clean hierarchy, proof chip, calmer layout, less visual noise

brandPersonality=luxury
- high contrast, refined type, subtle depth, minimal badges

brandPersonality=bold
- oversized type, strong color blocking, dramatic crop

If multiple personality words are provided, choose one dominant visual mood and
one secondary accent. Do not average everything into a generic layout.


AD-LIKE DESIGN DETAILS
----------------------

Every banner should include at least two of these ad design signals when space
allows:
- offer badge or proof chip
- intentional image crop or mask
- CTA button with strong contrast
- layered foreground/background depth
- diagonal split or asymmetric panel
- typographic contrast between headline and support copy
- subtle shadow, glow, or overlay that improves readability

Avoid:
- flat centered logo + centered headline + centered CTA in every format
- unstyled image dropped in the middle
- generic landing-page card layout
- too many equal-weight text blocks


LOGO STRATEGY
-------------

The company logo is locked when provided.

Reinterpreted logos are not logos. A similar generated mark, redrawn symbol,
stylized monogram, recolored version, simplified icon, decorative brand-like
shape, or fake logo does not satisfy the logo requirement.

Never redraw, recolor, restyle, regenerate, warp, crop into unreadability,
trace, simplify, add effects to, or replace the company logo. Use the exact
provided logo asset with original proportions and original colors.

If a logo asset is provided, every creative must include at least one visible
instance of the original logo. A missing logo, altered logo, fake logo,
invented icon, or brand mark borrowed from a reference ad is a failed creative.

If no logo asset is provided, use brand name text only. Do not invent a symbol,
seal, monogram, mascot, emblem, or abstract fake logo.

logo-strategy=auto
- choose best placement for contrast and composition

logo-strategy=full-color
- use full color logo on neutral or light/dark panel

logo-strategy=light
- use on dark image/color areas

logo-strategy=dark
- use on light backgrounds

logo-strategy=monochrome
- use when visual style is premium, minimal, or high contrast


CTA DESIGN
----------

CTA must look clickable:
- strong contrast
- readable type
- clear padding
- not hidden near edges

Social media formats are the exception:
- Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, social feed, social square, stories,
  reels, and any social media placement must never use CTA buttons.
- Do not use a button, pill button, rounded rectangle, bordered button, app UI
  control, or large clickable block in social media layouts.
- Express CTA as integrated ad copy instead: footer action text, underlined
  phrase, caption line, swipe/DM cue, offer line, or sticker text without a
  button container.

Display/banner formats may use button, pill, tag, or action block treatments.

For small display formats, CTA should be the cleanest object on the banner.

CTA PROPORTION RULE
-------------------

The CTA must be proportional to the exact format. It should never look like a
giant banner inside the banner, and it should never be so small that it feels
like a label.

Use these ranges unless a format-specific rule says otherwise:
- strip/leaderboard: compact right-side CTA, 10-14px label, max 30% width
- medium rectangle: 12-16px label, max 54% width, compact padding
- square/feed: 13-18px label, max 44% width
- story/portrait: 16-22px label, max 46% width, lower-third placement

CTA text should stay on one line. If the CTA is too long, shorten the copy
instead of enlarging the button.
